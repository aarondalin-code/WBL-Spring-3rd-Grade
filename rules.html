Game Format Time limits, innings, run rules

Field / Distance
60‚Äô bases, 46‚Äô pitching distance.
Game Length
2:00 time limit or max 6 innings.
No new inning after 1:45. Hard stop at 2:00.
Any inning starting at 1:45 is the last inning.
Official Game (Weather)
Official after 4 innings (or 3.5 if home team is winning).
Time Limit Ends Mid-Inning
If the game is stopped due to the time limit, scores revert to the last completed inning.
Run Limit / Mercy
Run limit: 5 per half inning, unlimited in the last inning.
Mercy rule: not in effect.
Sitting / Rotation
Every kid sits one inning before another kid sits twice.
No player should ever sit two innings in a row.

Pitching Limits, coach pitch option, mound visits
‚åÑ
Distance
Kids must pitch from 46 feet.
Limits
Max 75 pitches per game OR 3 innings.
Warm-Up Pitches
8 warm-up pitches in the 1st inning, then 5 after.
Hit By Pitch
If 3 kids are hit in an inning, pitcher must be removed.
Walk Limit + Coach Pitch Option
If a kid walks 4 in an inning, coach has the option to pitch the remainder of the inning to keep the game moving.
Coach must throw overhand from 46 feet.
Kids will continue to field the pitcher position while a coach is pitching.
If the kid removed has not reached pitch/inning limit, he may be brought back in to start the next inning.
Coaches will not ever start an inning.
Mound Visits
Coaches allowed 1 mound visit per pitcher per inning.

Fielding 10 fielders option, errors/advancement
‚åÑ
Fielders
Coaches may play 10 total fielders with 4 outfielders (no short fielder).
Teams may play with a minimum of 8 players in the field.
Errors / Advancement
On an error, players may advance a maximum of 1 base and defense has a free shot to get that baserunner out.
If an outfielder overthrows an infielder, baserunners get a maximum of 1 base (except runner on 3rd ‚Äî see Locked at 3rd).
End of Play
Play ends when the ball is returned to the pitcher‚Äôs mound.
Player Development
Coaches should play kids in both infield and outfield each game.
Every player should play 2 innings in the infield and 2 innings in the outfield.

Sportsmanship Umpires, dugouts, parent conduct
‚åÑ
Umpires
No arguing balls and strikes.
Cadet umpires ‚Äî bad calls are expected.
Only head coaches may talk to the umpires.
Cheering / Batter Distraction
Teams should stop cheering when the opposing pitcher starts his windup.
Batter could be called out if this rule is abused.
Player Conduct
If a player argues with an umpire, that player must be removed and sit a mandatory 2 innings in a row.

Kids must clean up after themselves and leave a clean dugout for the next game.

If parents are arguing or heckling an umpire, they will be asked to leave the field and sit in their car. There will be no exceptions to this rule.
